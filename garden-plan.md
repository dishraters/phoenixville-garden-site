Garden size: **18 ft deep × 24 ft wide**  
Orientation: **North/back at top, south/front at bottom**  
Goal: productive beginner-friendly vegetable + herb + pollinator garden with squash controlled.

## 3. Concise planting guide

### Tomatoes
- Plant spacing: **24–30 inches apart**.
- Put all 3 in the **back/north row**.
- Use cages or stakes from day one.
- Add basil nearby, but do not crowd the tomato stems.
- Prune lower leaves once plants establish to improve airflow.

### Peppers
- Plant spacing: **18–24 inches apart**.
- Keep poblano and jalapeño in the sunny back row beside tomatoes.
- Stake lightly if plants lean after fruiting.

### Eggplant
- Plant spacing: **24–30 inches apart**.
- Use **2 plants if you want easier maintenance**, 3 only if the row is roomy.
- Keep on the back/right side in full sun.

### Zucchini
- Plant spacing: **3–4 ft per plant**.
- Two zucchini is enough. Do not add more.
- Put one left-middle and one right-middle so they do not shade herbs.
- Harvest small and often to keep plants productive.

### Spaghetti squash
- Plant spacing: **4–6 ft vine zone**.
- Put it on the far left edge/corner and train vines outward.
- Do not let it run through the tomato/pepper row.

### Herbs
- Basil near tomatoes, about **12 inches apart**.
- Parsley in the front-middle for easy picking.
- Oregano/thyme along front or side border as low herbs.
- Mint must stay in a container only. Never plant mint directly in the bed.

### Flowers
- Marigolds/zinnias along the front edge as a pollinator border.
- Optional hanging baskets add color without using vegetable space.

### Care notes
- Full sun target: **6–8+ hours/day**.
- Mulch after planting to reduce weeds and hold moisture.
- Water deeply and regularly during Phoenixville summer heat.
- Keep 2–3 ft access paths clear for harvesting.
- Check squash weekly and redirect vines before they take over.

## 5. What to plant where table

| Garden zone | What goes there | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Back/north left | 3 tomato cages: San Marzano, cherry, slicer + basil | Tall plants stay north so they do not shade the bed |
| Back/north middle | Poblano + jalapeño | Full sun, easy to harvest beside tomatoes |
| Back/north right | 2–3 eggplants | Warm, sunny, upright plants fit the back row |
| Far left edge/corner | 1 spaghetti squash | Vines can trail outward instead of invading the garden |
| Middle left | 1 zucchini | Big plant gets room without crowding tomatoes |
| Middle right | 1 zucchini | Balanced spacing and easier harvest access |
| Front/south edge | Marigolds/zinnias | Pollinator border and visual appeal |
| Front-middle | Parsley | Easy kitchen access and lower growth habit |
| Front/side border | Oregano/thyme | Low herbs stay accessible and do not shade crops |
| Outside bed | Mint pot, hanging baskets, bay tree planter | Keeps aggressive mint contained and adds beauty without crowding |

## Hard rules

- Do not overplant squash: **2 zucchini + 1 spaghetti squash is the max**.
- Tomatoes need cages/stakes.
- Keep eggplant and peppers in the sunny back row.
- Keep herbs easy to reach near the front.
- Use flowers as a pollinator border.
- Mulch and water consistently.
