Plant key

  1. San Marzano tomato — caged, back/north left
  2. Grape/cherry tomato — caged, back/north left-center
  3. Slicing/sandwich tomato — caged, back/north center
  4. Poblano pepper — back row
  5. Jalapeño pepper — back row
  6. Eggplant — 2–3 plants, back/north right
  7. Spaghetti squash — far left edge, vines outward
  8. Zucchini — left-middle
  9. Zucchini — right-middle
  10. Parsley, oregano/thyme, marigolds/zinnias — front/south
  11. Mint, hanging baskets, bay tree — containers only/outside

Planting rules

Tomatoes, peppers, and eggplant stay in the back/north row. Herbs and flowers stay low and easy to reach at the front/south edge.

Squash control

Two zucchini and one spaghetti squash is enough. Put spaghetti squash on the far left edge and redirect vines outward every week.

Care rhythm

Mulch after planting, water deeply in summer heat, cage tomatoes immediately, and keep 2–3 ft access paths clear for harvesting.

Season roadmap

Shopping list

Vegetables

3 tomato starts, 3 cages/stakes, poblano, jalapeño, 2–3 eggplants, 2 zucchini, 1 spaghetti squash.

Herbs

Basil, parsley, oregano and/or thyme, plus mint for a separate container only.

Flowers

Marigolds and/or zinnias for the front border; optional 4 hanging baskets.

Containers

Mint pot, optional large bay-tree planter, optional bay tree.

Supplies

Compost, mulch, drip/soaker hose, garden twine/clips, plant labels.
